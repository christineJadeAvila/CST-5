About Me

I'm Christine Jade V. Avila, 20 years old. I like coding. I started coding when I was 17. It all started when I am casually browsing YouTube and watched a vlog about side hustles, and coding was mentioned as one of them. However, it wasn't the kind of coding like programming, but rather encoding, but, still, after watching the vlog, I googled about coding and wasn't really into it at first because it seemed difficult and challenging. So, I ignored it for a few days.

Then one day, while I'm cleaning the house, I turned on the TV and played random videos on YouTube. That's when I came across a video titled 'Don't Be A Programmer'. I paused my cleaning and focused on watching the video. It was about being a programmer, a problem solver, a creator, an innovator. The video is a partnership with IBM to talk about Call for Code a global initiative that calls developers to build impactful and have a positive change across the world through their code. After watching that video I wanted to be a programmer, and I started googling about how to become a programmer.
